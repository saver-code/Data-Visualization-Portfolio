Redesigning John Snow Cholera Map

Project Overview: This project reimagines John Snow’s seminal work on the 1854 Soho cholera outbreak, bridging the gap between historical storytelling and modern geospatial analysis. The redesign visualizes the same dataset through two distinct lenses:

Narrative: The artistic visualization illustrates the methodology behind the discovery, depicting John Snow’s data collection process and the physical location of the Broad Street pump. It uses a scatter-plot style to represent individual cases and saved locations relative to the pumps.

Geospatial Heat Map: The analytical visualization applies modern mapping techniques to the dataset. By utilizing a heat map, the design reveals "High Death Density" zones in red and yellow, visually confirming the clustering of fatalities around the specific water pump. This highlights the intensity of the outbreak more effectively than a traditional dot map.

Persuasive Infographic

The Vanishing Green

Overview of the Design Process This assignment represents a complete visual communication project focused on environmental awareness, specifically tracking the journey from concept to final design. It begins on the far left with a hand-drawn wireframe sketch, outlining the layout for charts and diagrams. This planning stage evolves into a set of polished, high-fidelity digital infographics titled "The Vanishing Green," "Roots of Life," and "Grow Back Better." The project effectively uses data visualization to narrate the cause-and-effect relationship between deforestation (showing 10 million hectares lost annually) and rising global Carbon Dioxide levels.

Data Visualization and Content The content breaks down complex environmental data into digestible visuals. It uses pie charts and bar graphs to categorize forest loss by industry (such as infrastructure and resource extraction) and by region (highlighting high loss in Africa and South America). The narrative pivots to solutions with a chart comparing 2025 plantation projects across nations like China and the United States. Finally, the "Grow Back Better" section utilizes a web diagram to illustrate the multifaceted benefits of trees—from oxygen production to soil protection—and promotes initiatives like seed bombing to restore the ecosystem.

Data Story

The AI divide in GTA

This presentation, titled "The AI Divide," examines the potential economic gap created by artificial intelligence within the GTA and Durham Region by contrasting two distinct career trajectories. It follows the stories of Jiya Sharma, an AI Engineer whose skills are augmented by technology, and Chris Anderson, a Logistics Supervisor facing job stagnation due to automation. The narrative establishes a clear dichotomy between those who "ride the wave" of AI and those whose routine tasks make them vulnerable to replacement, setting the stage for a discussion on future workforce security.

slides visualize this disparity through an "Earning Divergence" graph, which shows Jiya’s income nearly doubling to $150k over three years while Chris’s salary stagnates at $58k. This financial gap is further explained by a "Risk Matrix" that categorizes Jiya’s complex, problem-solving role as having "High Complementarity" with AI, versus Chris’s "Low Complementarity" role that relies on repetition. The presentation concludes with quotes emphasizing that the primary risk to workers is not AI itself, but rather being replaced by individuals who know how to utilize AI tools effectively.
